How the AI steps to calories converter works

Our intelligent calculator uses a combination of step length estimation (based on gender and height) and MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) values to give you a realistic calorie burn. Unlike generic calculators, this tool adjusts for walking speed: slow (2.8 MET), moderate (3.5 MET) and fast (4.3 MET). Studies show that walking 10,000 steps can burn anywhere between 300–600 kcal depending on weight, pace and height. The AI behind this tool uses the formula calories = MET × weight(kg) × time(hours). First, steps × step length gives distance, then time = distance / speed. The result is a science‑backed estimate.

Why 10,000 steps is a popular goal

The 10,000 steps target originated from a 1960s Japanese pedometer campaign, but it remains a solid daily goal for general health. For weight loss, combining step count with intensity (fast walking) increases afterburn. Our converter shows that 10k steps at moderate pace for a 70kg person burns roughly 350–450 kcal. But if you increase to fast walking, you can burn up to 500 kcal. This is equivalent to a small meal.

MET formula explained

MET stands for metabolic equivalent; 1 MET = resting energy expenditure. Walking at 3–4 mph typically equals 3.5 MET. By multiplying MET with your weight (kg) and hours walked, you get net calories. Our tool automatically computes walking time based on your step length and speed. Height is used to calculate average step length: women: height × 0.415, men: height × 0.43. If height is omitted, we use a default (165 cm).
